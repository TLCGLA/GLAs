Guided Learning Activities

 




Evaluating Online Sources


Introduction


In this activity, you will learn how to select suitable online sources for your research. A PowerPoint presentation will show you the main characteristics of reliable evidence and examples of websites that offer reliable evidence and those that do not. Along with the PowerPoint, you will apply the concepts by doing some exercises.

By the end of this activity, you should be able to:
  • Explain the main characteristics of reliable evidence and recognize their presence or absence in selected online sources.
  • Determine whether specific online sources qualify as reliable evidence.
  • Select online sources that provide reliable evidence.


Let's get started!


If at any time you need help please ask a tutor.

.

Step 1:

First you'll need to make sure you have the handouts for this activity. If you don't, ask a tutor to help you.

Step 2:

Watch this PowerPoint presentation on how to evaluate an online source. Pay particular attention to the concepts of accuracy, authority, objectivity, currency, and coverage, because you will be asked to apply them after you are done with the PowerPoint.

Step 3:

Now you are going to practice with some examples as a warmup activity. Please click on the links below and evaluate them according to their accuracy, authority, objectivity, currency, and coverage. Write your comments on a piece of paper, so you can later discuss them with a tutor.

  • http://www.epa.gov/climatechange
  • http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_warming  (Tip: note the disclaimer at the bottom of the homepage)
  • http://www.globalwarming.org

STOP and review your comments with a tutor.

Step 4:

It's time to move to the worksheet. Please find it in your handouts and complete the questions in section A. Note that the exercises say that you should work with a partner.  In the workshops, students work with partners, but for this GLA, you will have to do the exercises by yourself.  Do use a tutor as your partner at any time if you need help. (The links on the exercise are listed below for you.)

  • http://www.solutionsforclimatechange.com/natural-resources/species-biodiversity
  • http://www.greenpeace.org/usa/campaigns/global-warming-and-energy
  • http://www.ncdc.noaa.gov/oa/climate/globalwarming.html

STOP and review your worksheet with a tutor.

Step 5:

Again on the worksheet, please complete section B, where you will apply what you are learning by researching a topic. (The link on the exercise are listed below for you.)

  • www.canyons.edu/library

Step 6:

The last part of the activity is to reflect on what you have learned. Please go to the student self-reflection (Section C) in the worksheet and complete it.

Step 7:

Review your work with a tutor.

That's it. You are done! Thank you for completing this guided learning activity.
