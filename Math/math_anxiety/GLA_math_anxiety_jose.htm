Math Anxiety


Introduction


Part of doing well in any discipline is being physically and mentally ready for it. Students who have physical or mental symptoms of stress find it difficult to learn the material they are studying. Sweaty palms and loss of concentration are just two common symptoms of anxiety.

Sometimes we can identify the source of the anxiety. Some people feel anxious in small spaces. This is called claustrophobia. Others feel anxious in open spaces. This is called agoraphobia. There are many, many more triggers for anxiety, but for this workshop we will focus on a common source of anxiety for students: mathematics.

By the end of this activity, you should be able to:
  • Identify your own sources of math anxiety
  • Generate some strategies to counteract your anxiety


Let's get started!


If at any time you need help, please ask a tutor.

Step 1:

First you'll need to make sure you have the handouts for this activity. If you don't, ask a tutor to help you.

Step 2:

View this discussion on math anxiety. Be sure to have the worksheet with you, so that you can answer the reading comprehension questions in it as you go through the material.

Step 3:

Complete the self-reflection in the handouts.

Step 4:

Review your work with a tutor.
