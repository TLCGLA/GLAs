Relative Pronouns:  Possessive
•Samples:
•The lady whose house we bought was a strange person. She could tell about your past life just by looking at you.
•
•The student whose homework you copied hadn’t been to any classes, so you got everything wrong!
•
•“Whose” starts a relative clause and it is a possessive pronoun.  In the first sentence, “whose” refers to the lady. In the second, “whose” refers to the student.
