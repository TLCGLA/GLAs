•Original Text
•“Social anxiety disorder involves a fear of interacting with others or being in a social situation and is one of the most common phobias people experience.  People with social anxiety disorder are afraid of being evaluated in some negative way by others, so they tend to avoid situations that could lead to something embarrassing or humiliating.  They are very self-conscious as a result” (547).

•Applying the Concepts
•Can I think of an example of someone with social anxiety disorder? •One of my classmates had to give a speech in psychology.  She was so nervous that she could barely express her ideas to the class.  She finished quickly and almost ran back to her seat. •How might I diagnose a patient with social anxiety disorder? •I would ask the patient how s/he feels in social situations.  I would ask if s/he feels judged.  I would ask if s/he ever feels stage fright or fear of public speaking.
