Switching Costs
•What is the problem with multitasking? 
•For one, we don’t actually do two or more complex tasks at the same time.  Rather, we switch rapidly back and forth between those tasks.
•When we switch tasks, we experience two different switch costs.
•We take time to adjust to the new mental task
•We carry over aspects of the previous mental task
•In other words, we lose time whenever we switch between tasks.  (The more complex the task, the more time we lose.)
•The amount of time we lose may seem small, but it adds up quickly (sometimes as much as 40% of a person’s productive time).
