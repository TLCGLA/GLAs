Effects of Multitasking
•Today’s students are highly likely to multitask, especially through technology
•In fact, one large survey found that 80 percent of college students admit to texting during class; 15 percent say they send 11 or more texts in a single class period.
•The problem is that students overestimate their ability to pay attention to more than one thing at once.  They believe they can listen to their professor and send a text message, but the vast majority can’t do both effectively.
•When students multitask, they generally experience the following effects:
•Assignments take longer
•The mental fatigue of starting and stopping a task leads to more mistakes
•Students are less likely to remember the material well.
•Students are more likely to get lower grades.
