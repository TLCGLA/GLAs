Run-on
Betsy Ross was a seamstress who sewed the first American flag, in addition, she sewed ruffles on George Washington’s shirt cuffs.
Fixes
#1. flag. In addition, she
#2. flag; in addition, she
#3. flag; in addition, she (“in addition” is a transition)
#4. flag, and she
#5. No answer possible. The clauses are equal so it is impossible to make one subordinate.
INSTRUCTOR: Here are the five different ways we can fix this run-on. Where would we put the period?  [wait for response--after “flag.”] Where would we put the semicolon? [wait for response--after “flag.”] And for number 3, what would be a good transition word to use? [wait for response--some discussion will ensure, hopefully leading them to realize that the transition word is already there. This is a good opportunity to help them understand that transition words like “in addition,” “however,” etc. often disguise a run-on, and how the semicolon is necessary.] For number 4, what would be a good coordinating conjunction to use? [wait for response, comma and “and.”] And finally, for number 5, what would be a good subordinating conjunction to use? [this may also stump students. Remind them that when two clauses are equal, it may be hard to make one dependent or subordinate. Therefore, you can’t join them this way.
