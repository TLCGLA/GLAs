Example 1:  Responding to a Quote
´Quote:  “Man say to his woman:  I got me a dream.  His woman say: Eat your eggs” (Hansberry 1568).
´Sample Response:  I feel bad for Walter because I want him to be able to realize his dream.  But I don’t like how he uses the term “man” and “woman” here as if all men and women are in the same situation as he and his wife.  I also don’t like how he uses the phrase “his woman,” as if the woman is the property of the man.
´Response Explanation:  The response is the student’s initial reaction to the quote.  However, it could lead the student to an essay topic discussing marital relations in A Raisin in the Sun.  As you can see, the language is very casual, so the student wouldn’t want to use these exact words in his/her essay, even though s/he might use some of the ideas.
