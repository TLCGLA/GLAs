Correct with Subordinating Conjunctions
•Example:
•Deborah checked all the doors and windows of her house, she left for a long vacation.
•These are independent clauses that are joined by a comma. Therefore, the example is a comma splice.
•Corrected:
•Deborah checked all the doors and windows of her house before she left for a long vacation.
•Before Deborah left for a long vacation, she checked all the doors and windows of her house.
•Understand this:
•The above sentences are joined by “before” instead of a comma.
•“Before” is a subordinating conjunction.
•Subordinating conjunctions are at the beginning of dependent clauses.
