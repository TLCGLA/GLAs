Comma Splice!
•When only a comma joins two independent clauses, it is a comma splice.
•The following sentences are examples of comma splices. Read carefully to see how only a comma (,) joins two independent clauses.
1.I was hungry, I looked for an In-N-Out.
2.The clouds promised rain, it did not rain.
3.Many students are from foreign countries, they are here to study American culture.
4.
•What did you notice about these sentences?
1.Each sentence has 2 subjects (underlined) and two verbs (bold).
2.Each clause has a complete thought.
3.The clauses are joined only by a comma.
•
•Therefore, they are comma splices.
