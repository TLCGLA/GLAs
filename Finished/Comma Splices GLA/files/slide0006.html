Dependent Clauses
•Because John was sick yesterday. After he got home. Before he went to bed.
•Now look again:  There is a subject and a verb, but not a complete thought. Why?
•
•Notice the words at the beginning of each clause: because, after, before. These words are called subordinating conjunctions.
•When a subordinating conjunction begins a clause, it is called a dependent clause or a subordinate clause.
•
•All three of the above sentences begin with subordinating conjunctions, so they are all dependent clauses.
