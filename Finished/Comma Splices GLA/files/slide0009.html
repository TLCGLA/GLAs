Joining Two Independent Clauses
•In the earlier slides, you learned that a comma can join a dependent clause with an independent clause. That is fine!
•Now, read this:
•It rained hard last night, the roads were flooded by the unexpected rain.
•
•See what happened:
•The two clauses are joined by a comma.
•Neither of them is a dependent clause. Each one is an independent clause.
•This is a mistake. The mistake is called a comma splice!
