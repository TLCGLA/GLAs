Show and Tell
 When we tell someone information, we give them the information directly without giving them the opportunity to interpret it. 
 Example:  The professor was upset.
 
 When we show our information, we provide details and let the readers interpret them for themselves.
 Example:  The professor raised his voice and slammed his books on the table.
 
 We can combine both options, which gives readers a thorough picture of the information the we want to present.
 Example:  The professor raised his voice and slammed his books on the table; in short, he was upset.
