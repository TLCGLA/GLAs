Use your senses
 Tell your readers how your subject looks, smells, sounds, tastes, and feels.  Help the readers experience that subject for themselves.
 Carefully select which senses to use for which topic.  If you are writing about garbage, for example, you may not want to tell readers how the subject smells or tastes. 
 Sensory descriptions can capture the readers’ attention and make them more interested in your topic.  There are a few really great places to use sensory descriptions, like the introduction of an essay and in explaining examples.  (Of course, you can use sensory descriptions elsewhere, too!)
