WHEN NOT TO USE A COMMA
•Don’t use commas if the sentence loses important meaning when you take out the interrupter.
•Incorrect:  The drunk driver, who hit me, should lose his license. 
•Correct:  The drunk driver who hit me should lose his license.
•
•Don’t use a comma to separate the subject from its verb.
•Incorrect:  The girl who always wears blue, has chicken pox and will be absent all week.
•Correct:  The girl who always wears blue has chicken pox and will be absent all week.
