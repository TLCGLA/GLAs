Correcting Coordinating Conjunction Fragments
•There are two options for correcting coordinating conjunction fragments.  They are:
•Remove the coordinating conjunction
•Join the fragment with an independent clause
•
•Example:
•Andy was disappointed that the lion was asleep. 
•Andy couldn’t wait to see the lion, but he was disappointed that the lion was asleep.
•
•Explanation:
•In the first example, we removed the coordinating conjunction “but.”  Now, the sentence expresses a complete thought and can stand on its own.
•In the second example, we added an independent clause.  It has a subject (“Andy”), a verb (“wait”), and it expresses a complete thought.
