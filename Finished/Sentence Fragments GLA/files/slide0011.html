Subordinate Clause Fragment
•Each sentence must include an independent clause to be the main clause of the sentence.
•An independent clause includes a subject, verb, and complete thought.
•Clauses that begin with a subordinating conjunction (after, because, when, etc.) are dependent.  They cannot stand on their own.
•
•Example:
•When we saw something on the road.
•
•Explanation: 
•This clause begins with a subordinating conjunction (“when”), which makes the clause dependent.  It cannot function on its own; it is a subordinate clause fragment.
