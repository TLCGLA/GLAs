WHAT IS PROOFREADING?
  •   Proofreading is one of the final stages of the writing process.
  •   It is the process of carefully checking and assignment to look for spelling, grammar, and formatting errors.
  •   Students should save enough time before an assignment is due to proofread carefully
  •   If an assignment is submitted with errors, it damages the student’s credibility as a writer.
4
HOW DO I PROOFREAD?
5
STRATEGY 1:  TIME
  • Give yourself some distance from your assignment.
  • Once you’ve created a draft you’re happy with, leave the draft alone for a few hours (or better yet, a full day) before proofreading.
  • This means it’s very important to avoid procrastination.  If you finish your assignment right before it is due, there won’t be enough time to proofread well.
6
STRATEGY 2:  DON’T RELY ON SPELL CHECKERS
  • Spell checkers may miss words that are used incorrectly, even if they are real words.
  • For example, it will mark the word “thought” as correct even if the writer meant “though.”


  • Are all of the below words spelled correctly?
  • I have to do a through job on my lab report because I defiantly want to do well.
7
STRATEGY 3:  USE YOUR RESOURCES
  • Use your resources, such as dictionaries and handbooks.
  • They can help you find the correct spelling or identify grammar errors.


  • Where would you look to find out if you should use “its” or “it’s” in the below sentence?
  • I dropped my phone yesterday.  Now, (its/it’s) screen is cracked.
8
STRATEGY 4:  USE SEARCH FUNCTION
  • Use your computer’s search function to find errors that you know you make.
    • PC:  Ctrl+f
    • Mac:  Command+f
  • For example, if you struggle with there/they’re/their, you can search each of those words to check that you’ve used them correctly.
9
STRATEGY 5:  READ OUT LOUD
  • Read your assignment out loud, or ask a friend to read it to you.
  • Listen carefully for errors or awkward-sounding sentences


  • Let’s read the below sentence out loud:
  • I smelled the oysters coming down the stairs for dinner.
10
STRATEGY 6:  PRINT
  • Print out your assignment.  Often, it is easier to find errors on a printed copy of the work.
11
STRATEGY 7:  READ WITH A RULER
  • Normally, when we read our eyes skim over the page.  We read too quickly, which means that we miss errors.
  • Read with a ruler or piece of paper under each line of text.
  • This will force your eyes to slow down and focus on one line at a time.
12
STRATEGY 8:  READ BACKWARDS
  • This is another strategy that will help you slow down your reading.
  • When we read our own work, we know the content very well.  We may read what we expect to see on the page rather than what’s really there.
  • Read the assignment backwards, sentence by sentence.
  • This requires you to focus on the spelling and grammar of one sentence at a time.
13
STRATEGY 9:  KNOW YOUR MISTAKES
  • As writers, we tend to make the same mistakes over and over.
  • Check for mistakes you know you make.
  • If you struggle with comma splices, for example, read the assignment looking specifically for comma splices.


  • If you need help with grammar, see a TLC tutor or help yourself to a handout.
14
STRATEGY 10:  REVIEW CITATIONS
  • Review your citations to make sure they are correct.
  • Be sure you know what citation style (MLA, APA, Chicago, etc.) you’re required to use.  Check your assignment or syllabus; if no style is listed, contact your instructor.
  • Correct citations are an essential step in avoiding plagiarism.  It is important that they are accurate.
  • If you need additional help or materials on citation, visit The Learning Center!
15
STRATEGY 11:  FACT CHECK
  • Double check facts, figures, dates, and quotes to make sure they are correct.
  • Your readers rely on you to provide accurate information; in addition, you may lose points on the assignment if the facts are incorrect.


  • Let’s check the facts in the below statement:
  • The two major political parties in the United States are Republicans and Democracy.
16
STRATEGY 12:  ACADEMIC TONE
  • Be sure your tone is academically appropriate.
  • Most college instructors look for a degree of formality from their students.
  • A quick way to make your writing more formal is to remove the pronouns “you” and “I” as well as contractions like “can’t” or “we’re.”


  • Let’s make the below sentence more academically appropriate:
  • College students should bring your books to class.
17
STRATEGY 13:  PROOFREAD MORE THAN ONCE
  • Be prepared to proofread more than once.
  • Look for one or two types of problems at a time.
  • This is another great reason to avoid procrastination.  Students need time to proofread their work well.

WHY IS PROOFREADING IMPORTANT?
19
WHEN PEOPLE DON’T PROOFREAD, THEY EMBARRASS THEMSELVES
20
WHEN PEOPLE DON’T PROOFREAD, THEY MAY NOT GET A JOB
  • Employers will often choose not to interview candidates with errors on their resumes.
  • Employers want candidates who can represent the company well.
  • This means that they candidate must show that they can represent themselves well by proofreading carefully.
21
WHEN PEOPLE DON’T PROOFREAD, THEIR BUSINESSES CAN LOSE MONEY
  • An Italian airline accidentally priced a flight from Toronto to Cyprus at $39, not $3,900.  The airline lost $7.72 million due to their error.
  • The Australian Parliament gift shop created mugs celebrating Barack Obama’s visit to Australia, but spelled his name “Barrack.”  They lost $2,000 in revenue due to the spelling error.
