Personal Responsibility and Initiative
•In an American classroom, you are expected to take personal responsibility for your work.  This means:
•Reading and studying extensively outside of the classroom. 
•To earn C’s, plan to spend six hours a week studying for a three unit class.
•To earn A’s, plan to spend nine hours a week studying for a three unit class.
•Coming to class prepared to work.  Bring your notebook, pen/pencil, any required readings for the day, and any other materials your professor requires.
•Knowing and following the policies in the syllabus.
•Asking the instructor questions when you don’t understand the material.
•You should take initiative to ensure that you get the best education possible.  If you need help, ask for it (more on Seeking Help in Part 5).
