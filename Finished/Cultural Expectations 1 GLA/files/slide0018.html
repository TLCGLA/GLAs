Grades
•Getting good grades is vital to student success.  Get a good start from the beginning of the semester!
•Participation counts. Students often receive grades for participating in class discussions. Failing to do so could imply that you are not interested. So participate!
•Review the comments on your papers closely.  Make sure you understand what they mean.  Then, learn how to correct any problems.  For example, if your instructor writes “cs” on your paper, it means that you have a comma splice.  It is your responsibility to learn how to correct a comma splice.
•A few basic tips for improving grades – plan, practice, revise, & proofread. Ask for help when you need it!
