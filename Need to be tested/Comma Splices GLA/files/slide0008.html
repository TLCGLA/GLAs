Joining an independent and a dependent clause
•Read the following:
•John was sick yesterday. After he got home, he had stomach cramps. Although he lay down for a while, he did not get better. He had to take some medicine before he went to bed.
•
•Understand this:
•
•When a dependent clause begins a sentence, you need a comma before the independent clause.  ( i.e. – ‘After he got home , he had stomach cramps.)
•
•When an independent clause begins a sentence, no comma is necessary before the dependent clause. (i.e. – ‘He had to take some medicine before he went to bed. )
