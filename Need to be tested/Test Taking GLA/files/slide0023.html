Keep your answers clear and direct.  Don’t add irrelevant information just because you know it!
Don’t be specific on dates or other facts if you aren’t sure.  Approximate (e.g., “In the late 20th century”) is better than incorrect (e.g., writing 1998 when the year was actually 1991).
If you can’t think of a good answer, leave space and come back to it later.  Ideas may come to you!
