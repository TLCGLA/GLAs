Specific Test-Taking Techniques
Look for your predicted answer.  If you find it and you’re sure, mark it and go on.  If not...
•  Avoid options with absolutes such as all, always, and never.  They’re rarely the best answers.
•  Notice opposites in the options.  One of them is often the correct answer.
•  Identify equally plausible or unique options.  If two answers are equally good, neither can be best.  If some of the options are similar, the different one may be best.
•  Watch for a global option.  It may be the best.
With multiple choice questions, evaluate the options!
(Center for Academic Success; Dunham, 2004; Nugent & Vitale, 2004; UNC School of Nursing)
