  • What are some psychological symptoms or changes that happen during stressful events?
6
Physical  Changes
  • Heart rate increases
  • Headaches and migraines
  • Frequent colds, sinus infections, flu symptoms
  • Constant feeling of tiredness
  • Allergy or asthma attack
  • Upset stomach, indigestion, heartburn, ulcers
7
Psychological Changes
  • May experience difficulty in your ability to think clearly (confusion, trouble concentrating, inability to remember things, poor problem solving)
  • May experience emotions such as fear, anxiety, depression, irritability, anger or frustration
  • May experience insomnia or difficulty with sleeping

  •  How do you react or respond  to stress on these levels?
  • Emotional
  • Behavioral
  • Physical
9
Emotional Reactions
  • Depression
  • Anxiety
  • Taking your stress out on others
  •     (lashing out, angry outbursts)
10
Behavioral Response
  •  Eating, drinking or sleeping too much
  • Zoning out in front of the TV or computer
  • Allowing negative thinking to take over
  • Starting bad habits such as smoking/drinking
  • Spending too much money
11
Physical Response
  • Blood pressure goes up
  • Skin problems
  • Back pain
  • Muscles tighten up and joint pain
  • Lowered immune system

How do you manage stress?
  • Stress Pattern Worksheet:  Question 6
  • How do you manage or reduce stress in your life?
13
Manage Time to Reduce Stress
  • Determine your peak energy levels of the day - schedule classes when youre most alert
  • Balance time and energy on things that matter
  • Follow a regular schedule - include study time, work, sleep and relaxation
  • Minimize stress by planning in advance
  • Avoid sleep deprivation - can lead to accidents, increased mistakes, anxiety, depression
14
Adjust your lifestyle to reduce stress
  • Eat balanced meals
  • Try to exercise regularly
  • Limit caffeine and sugar intake
  • Learn positive self-talk
15
Managing Stress
  • Stress is a personal perception: work to change your responses to stressful events
  • Learn relaxation techniques:
  • Mindful Awareness exercise -
  • 5 minute Breathing Meditation
  •      - Helps break up anxious thought patterns
  •      - Can be practiced even in a noisy area
  •      - Stress doesnt live in the present moment
  •      - Use your breath as an anchor to focus on the present
  •      - Calms down and centers the student
16
Seek Emotional Support
  •      In times of stress its important to be able to share your feelings and seek emotional support
  •  Recognize the potential support in your life (friends, family, classmates)
  •  Utilize personal counseling services at the Student Health Center
  •  Meet with your academic counselor
  •  Join community groups, student clubs or social groups
17
Use Coping Skills to Manage Stress
  • Coping with stress is essential for making your life more productive and enjoyable
  • Coping means learning how to:
  •      - Manage personal problems
  •      - Solve personal problems
  •      - Reduce stress and conflict
