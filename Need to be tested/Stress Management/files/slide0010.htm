Effects of Chronic Stress
•Chronic, persistent stress damages almost every body system we have, including:  digestion, reproduction, growth, tissue repair, and immunity.
•It can lead to mental health problems like depression and anxiety.
•It can also lead to cardiovascular disease, including heart disease, high blood pressure, heart attacks, and stroke.
•The National Institute for Mental Health estimates that 70-80% of doctor visits are for stress-related illnesses.
•Stress has a negative impact on memory, making it more difficult to prepare for exams.
•Stress also hinders other mental skills like creativity, concentration, and attention to details.
