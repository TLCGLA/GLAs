Math Anxiety
•Math anxiety is a learned fear that prevents otherwise intelligent people from performing well on math exams.
•Math anxiety uses up our working memory, which is our mental scratch paper that we use to hold numbers in our heads as we work through math problems.
•So, the more anxiety you feel, the less attention you can pay to math problems.
•To overcome math anxiety, practice completing problems under pressure.  For example, you can use a timer.
