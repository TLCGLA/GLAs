Why do people procrastinate?
•Chronic procrastinators have made procrastination a lifestyle that affects all aspects of their lives.  They don’t only procrastinate on school work—they pay bills or file income tax returns late.
•In the US, we don’t treat procrastination as a national problem.  We don’t really take it seriously, and we don’t call people on their excuses (even if we don’t believe them).
•Procrastinators may lie to themselves.  They’ll say, for example, “I work best under pressure.”  In fact, most people don’t work best under pressure and most major assignments require time, thought, and effort in order to do well.  Unfortunately, procrastinators are squandering one of their important resources:  time.
•Procrastinators actively look for distractions (like checking email).
•Some procrastinators fear failure (or fear success), and procrastinate to avoid facing the consequences of completing their work.
