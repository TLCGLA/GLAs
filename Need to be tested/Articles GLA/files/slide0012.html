Other Indefinite Article Rules
•When we use the indefinite articles (a or an), we give our readers information about the noun.  We tell them:
•The noun is singular
•The noun is a member of a large group
•The noun represents the whole group
•
•Let’s look at an example:
•A dragonfly can travel up to 60 mph.
•
•In this example, we are discussing one dragonfly (singular), but it is a member of a large group of all the dragonflies in the world.
•The dragonfly in our sentence represents all dragonflies.
