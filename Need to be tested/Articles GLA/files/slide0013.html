Where Indefinite Articles DO NOT Go
•Do not use “a” or “an” before a proper noun (the name of a person or place).
•Incorrect: Carolina wants to take her picture in front of an Eiffel Tower.
•Correct: Carolina wants to her picture in front of the Eiffel Tower.
•
•Do not use “a” or “an” before plural or noncount nouns.
•Incorrect: Susan bought a watermelons to make fruit salad.
•Correct: Susan bought watermelons to make fruit salad.
•Incorrect: The car needs a gas before we can drive to San Diego.
•Correct: The car needs gas before we can drive to San Diego.
