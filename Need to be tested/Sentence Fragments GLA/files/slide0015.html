Relative Clause Fragments
•Like subordinate clauses, relative clauses are fragments if they are not attached to an independent clause.
•Relative clauses begin with relative pronouns (who, whom, that, which, whoever, whomever, and whichever).
•
•Example:
•Fabio is my nine-year-old nephew.  Who loves to play video games for hours at a time.
•
•Explanation: 
•The second sentence is a relative clause fragment.  It begins with the relative pronoun “who” and does not connect to any independent clauses.
