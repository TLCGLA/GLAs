Academic Integrity in the Classroom
•Your syllabus may include information on cheating and plagiarism.  It is extremely important that you understand what counts as cheating and plagiarism.  American colleges take plagiarism very seriously; you do not want to have a plagiarism incident on your academic record.
•Different countries or schools may have a different understanding of plagiarism.
•In the U.S. college system, we avoid plagiarism by accurately citing all of our sources.  “Citing” means giving credit to the person who wrote the original source.  You can ask your professor or a TLC tutor for help learning how to cite.
•You may also want to take a workshop on Avoiding Plagiarism or one of the citation techniques (MLA or APA).  TLC provides handouts on other citation styles.
•Your handout includes COC’s Academic Integrity policy.  It explains what counts as plagiarism at COC.  If you need help understanding the policy, ask your professor or a TLC tutor.
•If you have any questions about plagiarism, cheating, or academic integrity, ask your instructor or a TLC tutor for help.  They are happy to help you!
