Mode 5:  Classification
 Arrange people, objects, or ideas into categories based on similar characteristics.
 First, develop meaningful categories.  (For example, biologists classify different species by the temperature of their blood, i.e. warm blooded and cold blooded species.)
 Then, make sure the categories follow an organizing principle.  (Like the temperature of blood, the type of food a species eats, or the location of its skeleton)
 Provide examples to fit each category.  (Like snakes as cold blooded compared to dogs as warm blooded)
 You may end up comparing and contrasting the categories to show how they are similar and different.
