3. TITLE OF CONTAINER,
¡If the source is part of a larger whole, that larger whole is the container
¡Examples: a journal is a container for an article and a TV show is a container for a TV episode
¡The container is crucial for locating the source; in other words, readers need the name of the container in order to access your information.
¡The title of the container is usually italicized.
¡It is followed by a comma because the following information describes the container.
¡A source may have more than one container.  For example, a journal article from an online database will have two containers (the journal and the database).  Or a television show on a streaming service (like Netflix) will have two containers (the show title and the streaming service).
¡If there is more than one container, complete steps 3-9 for the first container.  Then, repeat steps 3-9 for the second container.
