How to Set Goals
•Start with your dreams.  In your perfect world, what will you be or do in the future?
•Then, work to refine your dreams to make them realistic goals.  Use the acronym FOCUS to help you.
•Fit:  Your goal must fit your values, your character, and who you are as a person.  If you are uncomfortable with conflict, for example, you may not want to be a courtroom lawyer.
•Ownership:  Your goal should be one that you pick for yourself.  It should make you excited about your future!
•Concreteness:  You should be able to describe your goal in specific detail.  “To run faster” isn’t very concrete.  Instead, try:  “to run a five minute mile by June 1.”
•Usefulness:  Goals should serve a useful purpose towards helping you achieve your dream.  If you hope to work in international business, for example, you should start learning another language now.
•Stretch:  Select goals that stretch your current abilities.  Challenge yourself!
