Typical Parts of a Journal Article
uAbstract:  Summary of the article.  Read the abstract to decide if the information in the article will be useful to you.
uIntroduction:  Provides background information on the topic and introduces the authors’ ideas.
uLiterature Review:  A summary of past research or publications on the topic.
uMethod:  If the article describes an experiment, the method section will explain how the experiment was conducted. 
uResults:  Details the results of the experiment. Depending on your purpose, you may not need to read the Method and Results sections.
uDiscussion:  Explains what the authors learned from conducting their experiment.  This is one of the most important sections in a journal article.
uConclusion:  Sums up the main ideas in an article.  May suggest areas for further research.
uReferences:  Lists the works cited in the article.
