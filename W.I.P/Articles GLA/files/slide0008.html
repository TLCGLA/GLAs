Specific or General?
•When you are deciding what article to use, you must decide if the noun is specific or general.
•
•Let’s look at an example. 
•If I say, “let’s wash the car,” I’m talking about a specific car.  The listener and I both know which car we’re going to wash. 
•
•However, if I say, “let’s wash a car,” I’m not talking about a specific car.  I’m suggesting that the listener wash any car rather than one specific car.
