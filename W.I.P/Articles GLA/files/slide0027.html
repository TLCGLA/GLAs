When Do We Leave Out Articles?
•Do not use an article when generalizing about plural count nouns.
•Elephants are intelligent creatures.  (“Elephants” are plural count nouns.)
•You should always recycle used water bottles. (“Bottles” are plural count nouns.)
•
•Do not use an article when generalizing about noncount nouns.
•Time is money.  (Both “time” and “money” are noncount nouns.)
•Furniture enhances the beauty of a room.  (“Furniture” is a noncount noun.)
•
•Do not use an article before a gerund when making a general statement.
•Skiing is a winter sport.  (“Skiing” is a gerund.)
•The boy’s parents taught him that lying is wrong.  (“Lying” is a gerund.)
