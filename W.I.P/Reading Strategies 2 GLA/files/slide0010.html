Sample:  What it Means
uOriginal Text
u“College athletics programs have become more harmful than beneficial to colleges and universities, critics say. The increasing diversion of resources and attention to such programs, they contend, has come at the expense of the academic mission of most schools, which have gradually lowered admissions standards and tolerated poor academic achievement for the sake of successful athletics programs.”
uSource:  “Do College Athletics Play a Valuable Role in Higher Education?”
uAnalysis of the Text
uAthletics and academics are set up as enemies uWriter privileges academic performance over athletics uAssumes that money should be spent on the “academic mission” of the school uAssumes that spending money on the academic mission will ensure that high academic standards will be achieved uOverall, the writer of the passage assumes that academics and athletics cannot exist in harmony; to spend money on one is to take away money from the other.
