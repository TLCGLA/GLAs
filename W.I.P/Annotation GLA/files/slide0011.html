How do we know what the author thinks is important?
uMany authors give readers clues to show what the most important information is.  Below, you’ll find a list of some of those clues.
uDefinitions and Illustrations:  If the author takes the time to define or illustrate a term, it is probably important.
uRepetition:  If the author repeats an idea, it is probably important.
uThey tell you:  Occasionally, the author will tell you the information is important.
