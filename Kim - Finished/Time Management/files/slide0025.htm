Important
“Fire Fighter”
Tasks that are both urgent and important are usually crises or pressing problems.
They can cause stress.
Although completing these tasks are necessary, we don’t want to spend most of our time here.
“This Is What It’s All About”
Tasks that are important but not urgent are the most valuable tasks to spend time on.  They help you plan for your future, prevent future problems, and recognize new opportunities. 
Try to spend the majority of your time here.
Not Important
“I Can’t Say No”
Tasks that are urgent and not important include responding to interruptions, calls, and emails.  If you focus too much on these tasks, you will only meet short term goals.  You may also be at the whim of other people.
“Procrastinator”
Tasks that are neither urgent nor important are time wasters and busy work.  These tasks lead students to avoid their responsibilities.
Try to spend the smallest amount of time here as possible.
