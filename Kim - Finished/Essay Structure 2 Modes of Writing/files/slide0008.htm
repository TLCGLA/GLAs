Mode 2:  Description
 Use detailed, specific language to help readers imagine an object, a person, a place, an experience, etc.
 Use vivid vocabulary to enhance your description.  Instead of using the word “red,” for example, use crimson, scarlet, russet, or rose.
 Use your senses.  Give the reader information about how your subject looks, smells, sounds, etc.
 Be organized.  Rather than give the reader a jumble of information, make sure each sentence relates to the others.  Consider describing from top to bottom or left to right.  This will help the reader accurately picture your subject.
