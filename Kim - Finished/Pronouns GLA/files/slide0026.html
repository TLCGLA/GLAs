Relative Pronouns
•Relative pronouns start relative (adjective) clauses.
•Sample:
•The lady who was talking to me last night was my new neighbor. She told me something that I didn’t believe. She said she remembered her past life, which she spent in a jungle of Indonesia.
•
•“Who” (a relative pronoun) starts a relative clause. It is a relative clause because it has a subject and a verb, and it gives information about a noun, “the lady.”
•“That” (a relative pronoun) starts another relative clause. It gives information about “something” (what the lady told me).
•“Which” (a relative pronoun) starts yet another relative clause and gives information about the “past life” (of the lady).
•Some relative pronouns are: who, whom, whose, that, which, whoever, whichever, etc.
