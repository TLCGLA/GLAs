What it Means
•After summarizing the paragraph (figuring out what it says), determine what the paragraph means.  In other words, you should analyze the text. 
•To figure out what it means, try the following:
1.Suspend judgment.  Don’t immediately react to the writer’s ideas.  Instead, work on comprehending those ideas first.
2.Divide the text into parts and determine how those parts are related.  To do this well, you must pay attention to detail. 
3.Determine the writer’s values, assumptions, and unstated ideas.  What does the writer think or imply without stating it directly?
4.Look for patterns, such as the repetition of ideas, contrasting ideas, and anomalies (ideas that don’t seem to fit).
5.Experiment.  You will have to ask yourself questions and try out different ideas as you work on your analysis.  Your analysis will evolve as you continue to work on the text.
