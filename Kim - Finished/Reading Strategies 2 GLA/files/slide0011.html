What it Does
•After analyzing the paragraph (figuring out what it means), determine what the paragraph’s role in the entire reading is.  Why might the writer have included this paragraph?  How does it contribute to his/her argument?
•To figure out what it does, try the following:
1.Think of the paragraph as part of a whole text.  How does it relate to the rest of the reading?
2.Think about structure.  Why is the paragraph placed where it is in the whole reading?
3.Examine the writer’s strategies.  How does s/he make an argument?
4.Evaluate the quality of the argument.  Is it logically sound?
