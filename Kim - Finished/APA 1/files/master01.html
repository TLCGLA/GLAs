Formatting your paper in APA style means paying attention to mechanical details such as:
•Typeface
•Line spacing
•margins
•Page headers
•
•Double space the entire paper
•Also double space between the text and titles, headings and block quotations
•Double space the reference list and figure captions
•Indent the first line of every paragraph ½ inch
•Align the text to the left hand margin, leaving a ragged right-hand margin
•Page numbers consecutively starting with page 1

In-text citations help establish credibility of the writer, show respect to someone else’s intellectual property (and consequently, avoid plagiarism).
More practically, in-text citations help readers locate the cited source in the references page. Thus, keep the in-text citation brief and make sure that the information provided in the body of the paper should be just enough so that a reader could easily cross-reference the citation with its matching entry on the reference page; i.e., the body of the paper and the in-text citation together contains the author’s name and the year of publication.
Anytime you use information from a source, provide in parenthesis the author’s name and the date of publication (Brown, 2010).
If you quote the author “word for word,” no matter how small, also provide the page number or paragraph number (Brown, 2010, p. 100).

This information on this chart is taken from the APA Publication Manual.  It reflects citation format based on number of authors.
The purpose of a reference list is to help readers find the sources you used. Therefore, the reference list it should be as accurate and complete as possible.
All citations should be listed in the reference list.
References in order by the author’s last name, or first author’s last name if there’s more than one author.
Use the hanging indent paragraph style. Double space the entire reference list. That means the author’s last name is hanging out on the reference sheet. It’s a reverse indentation.
A references contain the following components:
•Author’s last name, first initial only, publication date, title of work, and publication data.
