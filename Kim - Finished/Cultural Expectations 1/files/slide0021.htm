Grades
•Getting good grades is vital to student success.  Get a good start from the beginning of the semester!
•Participation counts. Students sometimes receive grades for participating in class discussions. In order to earn a good participation grade, be sure to contribute to class discussions.
•Review the comments on your papers closely.  Make sure you understand what they mean.  Then, learn how to correct any problems.  For example, some professors will write “cs” on your paper to show that that you have a comma splice. It is your responsibility to learn how to correct a comma splice.
•Also, if you cannot read your instructor’s writing, be sure to ask the instructor what the writing says.
•A few basic tips for improving grades – plan, practice, revise, and proofread. Ask for help when you need it!
