OXFORD COMMA
•Grammar enthusiasts debate whether or not we should use the Oxford Comma (the comma before “and” in a list).
•Often, the Oxford Comma ensures that a sentence’s meaning is clear. 
•Example:
•Without Oxford Comma:  Highlights of Cara’s global tour include meeting the Queen’s head butler, a world class cliff diver and an enthusiastic interpretive dancer.
•With Oxford Comma:  Highlights of Cara’s global tour include meeting the Queen’s head butler, a world class cliff diver, and an enthusiastic interpretive dancer.
•Without the Oxford comma, it isn’t clear that we’re discussing three different people.  Instead, it sounds like the butler is also a cliff diver and dancer.
