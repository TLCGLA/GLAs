•If the poem/song is not divided into numbered parts or stanzas, use line numbers.  For the first reference, use the word “lines.”  After the first reference, just use the numbers.
•
•Example:
•Lucille Clifton’s poem “homage to my hips” glorifies large hips, stating “they don't fit into little / petty places” (lines 4-5).
•
•Note that…
•The in-text citation uses the word “lines.”  This indicates that it is the first quote used from that poem.
•If the writer quotes from the poem again, s/he would use numbers only, like this:  (6-7).
•The title of the poem is meant to be in lower case.  That’s how the author wrote the title.
