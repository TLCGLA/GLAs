Mixture Problems
Introduction

Whenever we are looking at a compound, like water, blood, air, or steel, we are looking at a mixture. As you can see from the examples, even very basic things are actually a combination of more basic elements. Mixtures are all around us and in us. Understanding their composition and answering questions dealing with how individual components combine in a mixture is a valuable tool, which not only will give you a practical problem-solving method, but provide you with a deeper insight into the nature of things.

Student Learning Outcomes

By the end of this activity, you should be able to:

  • Apply strategies for solving mixture problems using (Quantity) x (Rate)
  • Solve mixture problems involving quantities of different cost rates (cost per pound, cost per person.)
  • Solve mixture problems involving quantities of different percent rates (concentration rate, interest rate.)
