What is an object pronoun?
An object is the part of the sentence that receives the action of the verb.

I eat pizza every day.
< "Pizza" is the object.
She brings chocolates to class.
< "Chocolates" is the object.

Note that "pizza" and "chocolates" are nouns.

A pronoun is a word that can take the place of a noun. That's why it's called a PRONOUN.

I eat it every day.
< "it" is the object pronoun.
She brings them to class.
< "them" is the object pronoun.
