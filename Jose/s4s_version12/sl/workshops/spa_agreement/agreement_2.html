Agreement
In Spanish you have the same situation as in the "a book" and "some books" example. Words must agree!

Check this out:
Singular
Plural
a book   =
un libro
some books   =
unos libros
 
a house =
una casa
some houses =
unas casas

Why does Spanish have two forms of saying "a"? One is masculine and one is feminine. The same goes for the word "some". Words must agree! Let's Practice.
