Agreement
In English when you say "white house" you don't need to make "white" agree with "house". For example, if you are speaking about more than one house, you would NOT say "whites houses".

In Spanish you DO have to make "white" agree with "house" or "houses". Check this out:
the white house    =  la casa blanca
the white book      =  el libro blanco
the white houses   =  las casas blancas
the white books     =  los libros blancos
As you can see in the examples, "white" had to become masculine or feminine, singular or plural in order to match the nouns it was affecting.

The word "white" is called an adjective. Adjectives are words that modify nouns like "house" or "book". Other examples of adjectives are "big, green, fast" and "smart".
