The verb form "hay"
In English, when you want to say that something exists, you use "there is" or "there are", as in "There are students in the class".

In Spanish, a single verb form, "hay", means BOTH "there is" and "there are".
There is a book on the table
Hay un libro en la mesa.

There are some books on the table
Hay unos libros en la mesa.

Note that "there is" and "there are" are only used to indicate that something exists.
