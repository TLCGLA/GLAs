The Infinitive of Reflexive Verbs
Another way to tell whether a verb is reflexive is to check whether its infinitive ends in "se".

Reflexive Verb
 
Not Reflexive Verb
levantarse
peinarse
bañarse
 
levantar
peinar
bañar

The "se" ending means "oneself", so "levantarse" is to get oneself up. In contrast, "levantar" would be used to get someone else up.

When you conjugate these verbs, the "se" ending goes away and becomes "myself, yourself, himself" and so on (me, te, se...) in front of the verb.
