Paragraph Structure (Writing a Source-Based Body Paragraph)


Introduction


When writing essays, you often have to cite sources in the body of the essay. There is a specific procedure you need to follow to cite these sources correctly. This Guided Learning Activity will show you how.

By the end of this activity, you should be able to:
  • Compose a source based paragraph of 8- 11 sentences
  • Identify 2-3 main points that support a topic sentence
  • Integrate 2-3 direct quotations in a single paragraph


Let's get started!


If at any time you need help, please ask a tutor.

Step 1:

First you'll need to make sure you have the handouts for this activity. If you don't, ask a tutor to help you.

Step 2:

Carefully read the handout, “What is a Body Paragraph?”  Make notes and highlight questions that you have.

Step 3:

Read the “Building a Source-Based Paragraph” handout. You will use the quotes in this handout to write your own paragraph around a topic sentence that's included in the handout. (If you are curious, the topic sentence is "Changing gender roles in the workplace are causing difficult adjustments for American husbands and wives.")

  • With the topic in mind, think about what main points will support your topic sentence. Identify 2 or 3 main points you would make about it.
  • Once you have the main points, decide which quote you will use to support each main point. Select one quote for each main point.

STOP and review your worksheet with a tutor. As you look at your work with a tutor, ensure that the quotations are good choices to support your main points.

Step 4:

On the handout, write in your three main points (A, B, and C in the outline). Follow the instructions as you provide a supporting quote and commentary for each of your main points.

STOP and review your worksheet with a tutor.

Step 5:

Now that you have a detailed outline for your paragraph, it's time to put it all together. What you are going to do now is turn the outline into an actual paragraph. Put the sentences that you composed on the outline map into paragraph form, keeping them in order and making sure that each flows smoothly into the next.

Step 6:

Complete the self-reflection in the handouts.

Step 7:

Review your work with a tutor.

That's it. You are done! Thank you for completing this guided learning activity.
