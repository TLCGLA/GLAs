Vocabulary Building


Introduction


Most of your college courses require that you read various kinds of materials like textbooks, essays, articles, stories, and so on. These materials often contain words that are unfamiliar to you. To get the most from your reading, you need understand the new words you encounter in your texts. This workshop will give you some strategies that can help you meet this challenge.

By the end of this activity, you should be able to:
  • Use contextual clues (definitions, synonyms, contrast, etc.) to guess the meaning of unfamiliar words
  • Analyze words into their component parts (prefixes, suffixes, and roots) and use them as clues to a word’s meaning
  • Explain the benefits of “reading more and often”


Let's get started!


If at any time you need help, please ask a tutor.

Step 1:

First you'll need to make sure you have the handouts for this activity. If you don't, ask a tutor to help you.

Step 2:

As a warmup exercise, please find the handout called "Vocabulary Building Exercise" and fill-in the vocabulary chart in it. This exercise will start you thinking about your vocabulary needs.

Step 3:

Watch this PowerPoint presentation on how to improve your vocabulary.

Step 4:

Now it's time to put what you are learning into practice. Please find the handout called "Vocabulary Building – Worksheet" and complete parts A and B.

STOP and review your worksheet with a tutor.

Step 5:

Using the same worksheet, complete parts C and D. These exercises require working with a partner, so you will have to ask a tutor be your partner.

Step 6:

Complete the quiz and self-reflection in the handouts.

Step 7:

Review your work with a tutor.

That's it. You are done! Thank you for completing this guided learning activity.
