Proofreading


Introduction


Proofreading is the stage in the writing process in which the writer reviews the essay to check for surface errors that were inadvertently included. Because it is frequently the final stage in the writing process, proofreading is often omitted. Students may finish writing an essay late at night and may be too tired to review it. Students may have procrastinated in completing the essay and barely have enough time to print a copy before class begins. As a result, students are not submitting their best work and are not receiving the grades they expect. Sadly, some of the errors that frequently appear on a final draft could have been detected and corrected had the student taken the time to proofread.

This workshop will present several proofreading strategies and alert you to the areas in which frequent errors occur.

By the end of this activity, you should be able to:
  • Identify and apply key proofreading strategies
  • Identify common surface errors that occur in essays
  • Utilize resources effectively to locate help in proofreading


Let's get started!


If at any time you need help, please ask a tutor.

Step 1:

First you'll need to make sure you have the handouts for this activity. If you don't, ask a tutor to help you.

Step 2:

Please find the "Proofreading – Strategies" handout in the Supplemental Learning Workbook and read it carefully.

Step 3:

The next step is view this PowerPoint presentation on proofreading strategies.

Step 4:

Now find the "Proofreading – Trouble Spots" handout in the Supplemental Learning Workbook and read it. It lists common errors, with links for you to explore. The reference links listed on the handout are reproduced below for your convenience:

  • Spelling errors
  • Punctuation errors
  • Sentence boundary errors: Fragments, Comma splices and run-ons
  • Subject/verb agreement
  • Verb Tense Problems
  • Pronoun problems

Be sure to learn more about errors that are not clear to you by clicking on the resource links above and carefully going over their contents. You will have to apply these concepts in the next step.

Step 5:

Complete the proofreading worksheet. Use the handouts and the links as resources.

STOP and review your worksheet with a tutor.

Step 6:

Complete the self-reflection in the handouts.

Step 7:

Review your work with a tutor.
