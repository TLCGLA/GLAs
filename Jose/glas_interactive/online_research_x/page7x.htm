Guided Learning Activities

Comprehension Questions

Instructions:

Use the library databases to research a topic.
First, access the library databases as you did before. This is how you enter the databases: Go to the library at this site: http://www.canyons.edu/offices/library/. A new window will open. You will have to go back and forth between the two windows to complete this activity. On the library webpage you just opened, find the button called "Research Here" on the top button bar. Click on it to open the research menu. Inside that menu, you will see "Online Databases" as one of the options. Click on it to open the list of databases available in the library.
Second, choose one of the following topics for your research by clicking on it and complete the activity indicated for that topic. (Write your answers on the handouts.)
Search the database and find at least two relevant statistical charts or graphs. Print them out or list the exact source for the information found.
Search the database and identify and list two arguments on each side of the marijuana debate.
Search the database and identify one appropriate article and name a relevant Supreme Court case (made-up example: California vs. Student Speaking Mind).
Search the database and identify two appropriate articles and print out the first page, or cite the articles below in MLA format.
Third, discuss this activity with a tutor. Were you successful? What features of the database were easy to use? Did you encounter any problems during your search?
