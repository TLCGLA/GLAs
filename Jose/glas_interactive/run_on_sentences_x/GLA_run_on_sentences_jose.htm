Guided Learning Activities

 




Avoiding Run-on Sentences


Introduction


In this activity you will review some key components of clauses and sentences. This is important in understanding how to avoid run-on sentences. You will view two short PowerPoints, one on run-ons and one on independent clauses. Next, you will apply this information to correct run-on sentences on a worksheet. Then you will take a quiz, and complete a self-reflection.

By the end of this activity, you should be able to:
  • Identify the components of an independent clause
  • Correct run-on errors between independent clauses using periods, semi-colons, and coordinating conjunctions


Let's get started!


If at any time you need help please ask a tutor.

Step 1:

First you'll need to make sure you have the handouts for this activity. If you don't, ask a tutor to help you.

Step 2:

Find the handout called "Avoiding Run-On Sentences − Introduction" in the Supplemental Learning Workbook and read it carefully. As you go over the material, write down any questions that you have and ask a tutor about them.

Step 3:

Watch the "Avoiding Run-on Sentences" PowerPoint presentation. (Note: No need to click on the "Clauses" hyperlink since you will view that PowerPoint later.)

Step 4:

Now, view the "Independent Clauses PowerPoint." (Note: for this activity, view slides 1-5 only. This slide is titled "Independent clauses can be connected in a variety of ways.") As you watch the PowerPoint, write down any questions that you have and ask a tutor about them.

Step 5:

Use the "Five Methods to Correct Run-on Sentences" in the "Introduction" handout to complete the "Sentence Strips Activity." (Note: In the workshop, this activity is done with strips of paper containing the different part of each sentence. As a GLA, you need to correct the sentences on the space provided in the worksheet, without the sentence strips.)

STOP and review your worksheet with a tutor.

Step 6:

Take interactive quiz "Repairing Run-on Sentences." Important: Note which problems you got wrong and review them with a tutor before you close the screen.

Step 7:

Find the quiz in the handouts and complete it.

Step 8:

Complete the self-reflection in the handouts.

Step 9:

Review your work with a tutor.

That's it. You are done! Thank you for completing this guided learning activity.
